About Me

Hi! I’m a Postdoctoral Associate in Astrophysics, studying observational cosmology. I’m especially interested in the diffuse sky (things like zodiacal light, diffuse Galactic light, and the cosmic optical and infrared backgrounds) as seen with space telescopes. The Hubble Space Telescope is basically my best friend.

I got my start at Texas A&M University in 2016, where I studied Physics, and worked under Prof. Casey Papovich and Dr. Vince Estrada-Carpenter. With Vince’s endless patience, I learned how to code in Python and find my way in a research environment. A big turning point was the STScI Summer Internship, where I sharpened my skills and had a blast, getting to interact with interns from all over the world.

That journey brought me to Arizona State University, where I became a full-on Hubble expert, working with Prof. Rogier Windhorst and Dr. Rolf Jansen. I was also lucky enough to work on some of the very first James Webb Space Telescope images, like the one in the background of this page!

Mentoring and outreach are really important to me. I believe one of the best ways scientists can give back is by supporting undergraduate students and helping prepare the next generation.

Outside of work, I love good food (especially Puerto Rican and Korean!), exploring national parks, skiing and the San Antonio Spurs! My biggest priorities are my relationships with friends and family, my health, and doing meaningful science!
